Statistics

Start with this really nice book, A First Course in Bayesian Statistical Methods, by Hoff, which is designed to introduce Bayesian methods to the newcomer.

Refer to this acclaimed resource, Bayesian Data Analysis, by Gelman, Carlin, Stern, Dunson, Vehtari, and Rubin, in its third edition, which gives guidance to researchers interested in modeling their data using Bayesian statistical methods.

Get oriented with machine learning principles with this book, Elements of Statistical Learning, by Hastie, Tibshirani and Friedman.

Explore this upcoming release, Causal Inference, by Hernan and Robbins, to begin to understand how to get beyond association and correlation.

Andrew Gelman's blog, Statistical Modeling, Causal Inference, and Social Science, provides lively commentary and discussion on statistical modeling, especially with respect to using modeling languages such as STAN and JAGS

Computational Biology

Take a look at Atlassian's tutorial on how to set up a GitHub repository.

Check out Karl Broman's tutorial on how to set up a simple site using git, GitHub, Markdown, and Jekyll.

Lior Pachter's blog, Bits of DNA, describes issues on the forefront of computational biology and next-generation sequencing analysis.

Microbiology & Immunology

For an encyclopedic introduction to viruses of many sorts, consult Fields Virology. If you are just getting into immunology, take a look at Janeway's Immunobiology, which also contains many good figures for background slides. Stay up to date with current infectious disease outbreaks by subscribing to ProMED-mail.
